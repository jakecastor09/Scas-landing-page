Our Story

HISTORY OF ST. CHARLES AUGUSTINE SCHOOL OF BIÑAN LAGUNA INC.

When we saw that there was a felt need to put up a Preparatory school in the community, we inspired to start a small school by using a small lot (garage) as the site of this learning center. Establishing a school was not so easy as there are lot of factors to be considered. For us, little kids who got to school in their early ages should have the notion that schooling is a worthwhile and happy experience and not a boring plain learning alone. Thus, the very infant Kindergarten and Nursery was named “Little Wonders Learning Center”. It is a school that communes with the community and it is a learning place where neighboring mothers and grandmothers don’t have to suffer a long way to travel to send their children and grandchildren to school away from busy streets hazards and from violent laden society in other words, accessibility to the school is favorable to the students. In 1999 with the expansion made in the said school, words of encouragement to change the name of the school have been raised by the parents concerned and staff as well. Thus, LWLC is now named, St. Charles Augustine School of Biñan Laguna Inc. with the approval of the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on May 2, 2002. St. Charles Augustine School of Biñan Laguna Inc. is a non-sectarian institution owned by Reynaldo/Millette Casunuran, it is non-profit, non-stock institution situated at 170 Brgy. Timbao Biñan, Laguna. The school started its operation in the school year 1995-1996 offering Pre-elementary education with barely 1 room with 15 pupils enrolled in Kinder. On second year, the school opened complete Pre-elementary department Nursery, Kinder and Preparatory respectively. The population grew to 30, an indicator of community’s confidence with us. In the school year 1997-1998 government recognition was granted by the DECS for the Pre-Elementary May 21, 1997. When the Elementary level (Grades 1-3) was opened, its population rose to 45. By the school year 1998-1999 when the Elementary was opened (Grade 1-4) the student population became 65. On its fifth year operation (1999-2000) the student population rose to 70. The legal documentation for its registration was approved by the DTI on September 16, 1999. Then in the school year 2000-2001 SCAS - Pre-elementary department became fully operational. Government recognition was granted by the Department of Education for the Elementary level on April 17, 2001 respectively. An annex was opened on its eighth year of undertaking (AY 2008-2009) having 86 pupils. Thereafter, renovations and additional facilities were constructed in order to accommodate the increasing number of pupils. Additional lots were also acquired for further expansion.

Mission

S.C.A.S commits itself: in tapping and developing the child’s inherent talents and skills, in expanding his horizon, and in providing him the highest standard of education at a price affordable even to the below average-income families.

Vision

S.C.A.S believes that a child who is closely supervised and exposed to a stimulating learning environment, resources and approaches is enabled to learn develop his multiple intelligences and abilities and is encouraged to socialize with his peers gaining an optimum chance of attaining his academic goals and improving his personal relationship.
